← All posts Most software treats the audit log as an afterthought — a table you hope you never have to read. We built Marzy the other way around.

When an agent takes real actions on real systems, "what happened and why" can't be a guess. So every action Marzy takes records three things: the source it read, the reasoning it followed, and the exact change it made. Nothing is editable after the fact.

From cost center to feature

Compliance usually shows up as overhead — the work you do to prove you did the work. When the trail is automatic and complete, that flips. Closing the books, passing an audit, answering "who approved this?" stops being a project and becomes a query.

  • Every action links back to its source document.
  • Every decision carries the reasoning that produced it.
  • Every change is timestamped and immutable.

Why it builds trust

You don't trust an automation because it's confident. You trust it because you can check it. The audit trail is what lets a team watch a workflow run, understand each decision, and decide it's safe to let run on its own.

Export, always

The trail is yours. Every record and the full history export to CSV and PDF at any time. If you ever leave, you take everything with you — and we delete our copy on request.

An audit trail that's complete by default doesn't just keep you compliant. It's the thing that makes the rest of the automation possible.
