← All posts Everyone wants to point a model at their data and get magic. Then the model meets the data, and the magic meets reality.

The back office a team inherits is rarely clean. The same customer exists three times under three spellings. Phone numbers are missing. Dates don't agree across systems. A model handed that mess will confidently produce nonsense — and nonsense that takes real actions is worse than no action at all.

Reconcile first

Before a model ever runs, Marzy reconciles every record into clean, consistent rows: duplicates merged, missing fields enriched, formats normalized. Only then does the agent get to work.

  • Dedupe — collapse the same entity across systems into one record.
  • Enrich — fill the gaps from the sources that do have the data.
  • Normalize — one format for names, dates, phone numbers, amounts.

It's not a one-time step

Data doesn't stay clean. New records arrive messy every day, so reconciliation runs continuously. The structured, searchable layer Marzy maintains is what makes the rest possible — including answering questions about your data in plain language.

Clean data is the product

It's tempting to treat cleanup as plumbing. But the quality of every downstream action — every draft, every reconciliation, every answer — is capped by the quality of the data underneath it. So we treat the data as the product, and the agent as what it unlocks.
