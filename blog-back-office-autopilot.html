← All posts Every business runs on routine work nobody wants to think about — payroll, billing, reconciliation, records. It's the work that has to happen and rarely gets noticed until it breaks.

For most teams, that work is held together by a person and a spreadsheet. It scales by hiring. And the knowledge of how it actually gets done lives in someone's head. We think there's a better answer: hand the routine to an agent, and let people decide only what actually needs deciding.

Automation isn't a chatbot

The first wave of AI tools answered questions. That's useful, but it leaves the work where it started — with you. Marzy is different: it takes the action. From a payroll email to a filed pay run, it drafts the work, checks it against the systems of record, and completes it.

The hard part was never generating text. It was acting safely on systems that matter, with data that's usually a mess, in a way someone can later audit.

Earn trust, then automate

Nothing runs unattended on day one. Every workflow starts in review: Marzy drafts, you approve, and the action runs. You watch it work. Once a flow has cleared a few review cycles without edits, you can trust it — and it runs on its own, on schedule.

  • Draft — Marzy prepares the work from what arrived.
  • Review — anything uncertain waits in a queue with its reasoning.
  • Run — trusted flows execute on their own, in your timezone.

The point isn't to remove people. It's to remove the parts of the job that never needed a person in the first place.

Show your work

Every action keeps its trail: the source it came from, the reasoning behind it, and the exact change it made. An auditor can follow any decision end to end. That trail is what makes trust possible — and, as it turns out, it's what makes the whole thing pay for itself.

Where to start

The best first workflow is the one that's painful, repetitive, and well-defined. Payroll. Month-end close. AR follow-up. We start with a demo or a scoped pilot, embed an engineer to map how your work actually moves, and ship the first automation behind your approval. From there, it compounds.
